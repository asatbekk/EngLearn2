Pronoun

A pronoun is a word that replaces a noun. The pronoun does not name an object or person, it just refers to it. There are several features of English pronouns

Article

The indefinite article a/an is used only with numbered nouns in the singular: a girl, a pen. If a word begins with a consonant, we write the article a (a girl) and if the word begins with a vowel, we put the article an (an apple).

The verb to be

There is always a verb in an English sentence. And while in Russian we can say "I am a doctor", "Mary is beautiful", "We are in the hospital", in English this is not allowed: in all these cases there must be a verb to be after the subject. So you can remember a simple rule: if there are no regular verbs in a sentence, then you need the verb to be.

Present continuous

The Present Continuous Tense most often shows that the action is happening at the moment. Every English sentence has a subject and a predicate. In Present Continuous, the predicate consists of an auxiliary verb to be in the correct form (am, is, are) and a main verb without the particle to, to which we add the ending -ing (playing, reading).

Present Perfect

For example, I really love the old American musical Singing in the Rain. The title of the musical matches the first line of the song - Singing in the rain. And when you need to translate the phrase "in the rain" from Russian to English, your brain finds the right version in the rain itself, because the melody of the song sits firmly in it. And you know for sure that in the rain is not under the rain, not at the rain, but in the rain.

Past Simple

To form Past Simple, we add the ending -ed to the verb, if the verb is regular (work - worked, learn - learned, ask - asked). If the verb is irregular, we look for the form for Past Simple in the second column of the irregular verb table. If you start with an irregular verb, you may have trouble telling which verb is right in front of you. You will find the wrong verb in the table. If it is not there, it is correct.
